Video Game Sales Forecasting

Forecasting · Statistical Analysis

I learned gaming industry dynamics while analyzing 35+ years of sales data—watching the industry evolution from arcade machines to mobile gaming play out in the numbers.

The challenge was identifying which games would succeed in 2017 based on historical trends. I analyzed sales across platforms (Xbox, PlayStation, PC), regions (North America, Europe, Japan), and genres to determine the factors that actually drive success.

The most valuable outcome was translating statistical findings into clear business recommendations. I created visualizations that show exactly where to allocate advertising spend—accessible to marketing teams without requiring technical expertise.

This was my first forecasting project using Python. I leveraged GitHub Copilot to accelerate the learning process, which taught me how to work effectively with AI development tools—an increasingly essential skill in technical roles.

Megaline Statistical Analysis

Statistical Hypothesis Testing · Business Intelligence

I learned statistical hypothesis testing for this project—understanding concepts like p-values and confidence intervals. Through focused research and working with Copilot, I grasped not just the calculations, but the practical interpretation and application of these methods.

The project analyzed a telecom company's calling plan performance. I researched how telecom businesses operate, then worked through 5 datasets to understand how customers actually use their minutes, texts, and data.

The objective was straightforward: provide executives with a data-driven recommendation on which calling plan to promote. All the statistical analysis ultimately served one clear, actionable decision—exactly the kind of work that bridges technical analysis and business value.

I designed the analysis to be reproducible with updated data, not just a one-time deliverable. This distinction separates academic exercises from production-ready business solutions.

Instacart Market Basket Analysis

Exploratory Data Analysis · Customer Behavior

This was my first experience working with large-scale data—approximately 30 million grocery transactions from Instacart. The scale was challenging initially, but I learned to systematically break it down and identify meaningful patterns in customer shopping behavior.

I connected 5 different datasets to understand the full customer journey. Key questions included: When do customers typically order? What products do they purchase together? Which items do they reorder? I'd develop hypotheses, then validate them through data exploration.

The deliverable was a Jupyter notebook that walks through the complete analytical story—from raw transaction data to business insights. I designed it for product managers to understand immediately what customers are doing and why it matters for decision-making.

Working with real-world data meant handling missing values, inconsistent formats, and unexpected anomalies. Learning to clean and manage messy data professionally was as valuable as any specific insight from the analysis.

Movies & Shows Analysis

Data Analysis · Pandas Fundamentals

This foundation project focused on learning pandas fundamentals through hands-on practice with real streaming platform data. I worked through filtering, sorting, and grouping operations—building the core skills needed for any data analysis work. The learning approach emphasized practical application over theory.

The dataset had significant quality issues from the start: missing values, inconsistent date formats, duplicate entries. These pandas skills became foundational for every subsequent project. Once you can confidently manipulate data structures, you can work with any domain—customer records, sales transactions, web analytics, or operational logs.

The analysis itself provided insights for streaming content acquisition decisions. This reinforced an important principle: technical tools exist to serve business outcomes, not the reverse. The technology enables the analysis, but the business question drives the work.
